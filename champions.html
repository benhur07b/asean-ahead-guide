Guide · Champion

You are a motivator and guide — not an instructor.

The course teaches itself; your job is to get every participant registered, moving, and across the finish line. This page is everything you're accountable for before, during, and after an event.

Your role

What a facilitator owns

  • Running the standard agenda at every event, in every mode
  • Walking participants through ADA registration and enrollment
  • Capturing the complete participant list — no list, no event
  • Following up until every participant completes or the cohort deadline passes
  • Submitting attendance, completions, and documentation to the Program Officer
Onboarding first. Before your first event: finish the training yourself, complete the Champions orientation, commit to the data privacy and safeguarding guidelines, and get access to the tracking sheet and comms channels.

Delivery modes

Synchronous or asynchronous — know the rule for each

Synchronous

Complete everything live

In-person, webinar, or hybrid. The entire course is completed during the event — participants leave with all three completion requirements done. Plan at least two hours, run as two parts.

Read the synchronous playbook → Asynchronous

Introduce, then follow up

Recorded or live entry, self-paced finish. Every participant is on the tracking sheet with an assigned Champion, then follows a fixed Day 0 to Day 28 follow-up sequence to completion.

Run of show

The standard synchronous agenda

Every synchronous event runs in two parts. Part 1 takes everyone from registration through Module 1 — after that, participants may opt to finish the rest asynchronously. Part 2 continues live all the way to full completion of the training. Adjust the buffer, not the structure. Budget generously.

Lifecycle

Before · During · After

  1. Before the event

    Choose the delivery mode. Pre-register participants. Check the venue and connectivity (or set up the online session). Prepare materials and confirm devices — the course is mobile-friendly, but laptops work better for tests and discussions.

  2. During the event

    Run the standard agenda. Capture the complete participant list with all required fields. Get every participant registered and enrolled on ADA before the event ends. Troubleshoot the common issues: login problems, test submission, video playback on mobile.

  3. After the event

    Update the central tracking sheet. Check completion status weekly, send nudges to incomplete participants, and escalate persistent drop-offs to the Program Officer. Submit attendance, completions, and photos.

Data & safeguarding

The participant list — required fields

  • Full name and active email (must match the ADA account)
  • Region/province and institution (if any)
  • Beneficiary type: university student / TVET student / NEET youth / other
  • Gender and age (for disaggregated reporting)
  • Event, date, mode (sync/async), and facilitator/host
  • Status: registered → enrolled → in progress → completed → verified
Collect only what's listed. Follow the consent and data privacy guidelines from your orientation. When in doubt about what to collect or share, ask the Program Officer first.
