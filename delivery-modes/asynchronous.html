Delivery mode · Asynchronous

They finish on their own — but never alone.

Self-paced does not mean unsupported. Every self-paced participant has one assigned Champion and a fixed follow-up sequence from Day 0 to Day 28.

At a glance

Who this playbook covers

This applies to every participant whose completion type is self-paced, no matter how they entered the programme. There is no separate dropout list — a participant either finishes or is still being followed up.

DimensionValue for this modeSet when
Entry — how they do Part 1Recorded orientation video, or live for those who left after Part 1At registration — immutable
Completion — how they do Part 2Self-pacedFrom the event type, or the moment they leave at a Part 1 exit point
Two doors, one playbook. Open-call sign-ups who watch the recorded orientation, and Part 1 leavers from live sessions, are handled identically from Day 0 onward. The recorded orientation requires the same registration and pre-course evaluation before Module 2, so the reporting baseline is the same in every mode.

Day 0

What starts the clock

  • Day 0 is the Part 1 date — the day they completed the orientation, live or recorded
  • Every participant is assigned to exactly one Champion at registration, so no participant can exist without an owner
  • That Champion stays accountable until the participant is verified or dropped
  • Day 14 is the ideal deadline; Day 28 is the acceptable one

Follow-up sequence

Day 0 to Day 28, message by message

Templates live in the Resources appendix, in English with Taglish variants for participant-facing nudges. Copy, personalize the name, date, and progress, then send — and log the send as a dated fact in the tracking sheet.

  • Day 0 — Welcome Email + Messenger/SMS

    Course link, the three completion requirements, and their Day 14 target date. Send it the same day, while motivation is still fresh.

  • Day 3 — First nudge Messenger/SMS

    Phrase it as a question, not a broadcast: "How's Module 2 going?" A question invites a reply, and replies tell you who is reachable.

  • Day 7 — Progress check Email + Messenger

    Check actual ADA progress first. Congratulate the movers with a real number; re-link the laggards to exactly where they stopped.

  • Day 10 — Deadline reminder Messenger/SMS

    Give a remaining-time estimate, and name the two requirements people forget: the discussion post and the post-course evaluation.

  • Day 14 — Ideal deadline Email + Messenger

    Completers get congratulations plus the verification intake form. Everyone else gets the extended window to Day 28 and one direct question: what's blocking you?

  • Day 21 — Mid-window check Messenger

    Short check-in for those still active. Offer the next webinar's Part 2 as a guided finish — same course, same completion, live support.

  • Day 28 — Final push Email + Messenger

    The last structured follow-up. After this, individual chasing stops unless they respond.

  • After Day 28 — Light touch Monthly batch

    A monthly "the course is still open" note. No individual follow-up time unless the participant replies.

Triage

Three lanes — protect your time

From Day 7, every self-paced participant sits in one of three lanes. You don't assign the lane by hand — the tracking sheet derives it from the facts you record: last reply date and module progress.

Active

Replying or progressing

Run the full sequence. These are your best returns on time — keep the momentum going.

Stalled-responsive

Replies, but no progress

Diagnose the one specific blocker — login, device, time, or content — and solve that. Offer the webinar guided finish.

Unresponsive

No reply, no progress

No reply to two or more consecutive touches. Batch messages only. Spend no individual time here. Eligible to be marked dropped after Day 28.

Unresponsive is a routing decision, not a judgment. It only means batch messaging from here. They stay on the sheet, they keep their Champion, and they can rejoin any webinar's Part 2 at any time.

Weekly routine

Every Monday, for every assigned participant

  1. Pull ADA progress and record the facts

    Dates, module progress, last contact, last reply. Record facts only — the sheet computes day counts, overdue flags, and lanes for you.

  2. Send everyone's due sequence message

    Work down your assigned list and send whatever the sequence calls for that day. Log each send.

  3. Flag two things to the Program Lead

    Participants ready for verification, and systemic stalls. A whole student org stuck at Module 3 is one content or process problem — not thirty individual ones.

  4. Close the loop

    Mark dropped only where the criteria are met: in progress, past Day 28, and unresponsive. Log any new questions for the FAQ.

Record facts, not judgments. Officers enter dates and progress; the sheet derives the lane, the overdue flags, and the completion type. That is what keeps the data from drifting — there is no lane to forget to update.
