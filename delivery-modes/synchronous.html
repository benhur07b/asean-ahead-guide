Delivery mode · Synchronous

Live from start to finish.

Participants enter live and finish live. Done right, everyone leaves the session with all three completion requirements met — no follow-up chase needed.

At a glance

What "synchronous" means here

A pathway is the product of two independent dimensions. Synchronous is the combination of a live entry and a facilitated completion — webinar, in-person, or hybrid session, run straight through both parts.

DimensionValue for this modeSet when
Entry — how they do Part 1Live (webinar, in-person, or hybrid)At registration — immutable
Completion — how they do Part 2Facilitated (live with the cohort)Defaults from the event type; flips to self-paced if they leave after Part 1
One block, two parts. The standard agenda runs straight through in a single block of at least two hours — Part 1 about 45 minutes, Part 2 about 105 minutes. The full segment-by-segment tables live on the Champions page; this page covers how to run the mode around them.

Staffing

Never run a session alone

Role

Lead host

Runs the standard agenda, presents the intro, walks through ADA registration, and paces the modules. Keeps the block on time.

Role

Co-host / producer

Admits participants, monitors chat, troubleshoots ADA account issues one-to-one, maintains the live participant list, and records who leaves at the Part 1 exit point.

No single-person dependency. Hosting rotates across the Program Lead plus at least two trained Champions, so no session depends on one person being available.

Before the session

The weekly run-up

  • Friday — recap & announce

    Post the recap of the session just run, with the completion count as social proof, and announce the next one. Same fixed registration link as always.

  • Monday — partner push

    Champions seed campus channels and student-org group chats. Partner institutions forward to their students.

  • Tuesday–Wednesday — reminders

    Reminder posts and Messenger/Viber group seeding. Pre-load any partner-supplied participant lists into the tracking sheet.

  • Session morning — last call

    Last-call post and a reminder to all registrants: bring a device and an active email. Remind them that creating the ADA account beforehand saves about 20 minutes on the day.

  • Session day — confirm the room

    Confirm your co-host. Check venue, connectivity, and devices, or set up and test the online session. Have the intro deck, the ADA walkthrough with screenshot fallback, and sample discussion prompts ready.

Holiday rule. If the slot lands on a Philippine public holiday, move the session one day earlier that week and announce the change by Monday.

Running the session

At least two hours, straight through

  • Part 1 — Orientation & onboarding ≈ 45 min

    Program intro, ADA account creation and enrollment, the pre-course evaluation, and Module 1. The co-host handles account problems one-to-one so the agenda keeps moving.

  • The Part 1 exit point — announce it out loud

    Say explicitly that participants may leave here and finish the rest on their own. The co-host records exactly who leaves. Their completion type flips to self-paced and they move onto the self-paced follow-up sequence starting the same day.

  • Part 2 — Completion ≈ 105 min

    Modules 2–5, at least one discussion post, then the post-course evaluation and post-training survey. Everyone still in the room should leave with all three completion requirements done.

Leavers are reclassified, never lost. A participant who leaves after Part 1 is not a dropout — they are a self-paced participant from that moment, with the same assigned Champion and the same baseline data. Record the fact; the tracking sheet handles the rest.

Close-out

Same day, before you log off

  1. Export registration and attendance into the tracking sheet

    One row per participant, keyed by ADA account email. Every identity and reporting field gets set here — this is the only moment the data is cheap to collect.

  2. Set the completion type for every participant

    Facilitated for those who finished live; self-paced for Part 1 leavers. Mark no-shows. No participant is deleted or moved to another list.

  3. Send the Day 0 message to every self-paced participant

    Assigned Champions send it the same day, while motivation is still fresh — course link, the three completion requirements, and the Day 14 target date.

  4. Log new questions and issues

    Anything asked twice belongs in the FAQ. Anything that blocked the agenda belongs in your report to the Program Lead.

What to submit. The attendance sheet, post-event report, and the merged participant list — the full list with templates is on the Champions page.
