Guide · Partners & Hosts

You bring the people. We bring the training.

Universities, organizations, student orgs, and individual hosts make this program reach communities we couldn't reach alone. Here's how hosting works, and how we support you.

Partner types

Two ways to partner

Institutional

Universities & organizations

You bring the participants; we bring the materials, training, and facilitators.

You provide the venue (if in-person), recruitment, and local logistics. We provide the facilitators, the standard agenda and materials, tracking and reporting, and the certificates pathway.

Community host

Individuals & student orgs

Host your own event: in your school, org, barangay, or community. You follow the same standard agenda and submit the same data as any other event, and qualify for financial support when your event meets the minimum requirements.

Financial support for community hosts: PhP 3,000 for every 50 participants who successfully complete the course. Completion—all three course requirements—is what counts, not attendance.

The bar to clear

Minimum event requirements

Every event, every host—these six requirements qualify your event (and your financial support):

  • The standard agenda is followed
  • A complete participant/beneficiary list is captured with all required fields
  • Every participant is registered and enrolled on ADA before the event ends
  • A facilitator or trained host is present and accountable for follow-up
  • Completion is tracked until each participant finishes or the cohort deadline passes
  • Post-event data (attendance, completions, photos/documentation) is submitted to the Program Officer

How it works

From interest to reported event

  1. Express interest

    Reach out through the program channels with your proposed audience, location, and preferred format (in-person, webinar, hybrid, or an async intro session).

  2. Onboard as a partner

    Agreement or endorsement as needed (MOA for institutions), an onboarding call, and the branding and communications guidelines.

  3. Plan the event

    Set the date and format, confirm the venue and connectivity, and coordinate facilitator assignment. Pre-register participants so the list exists before the day starts. Plan ~3 hours for a full synchronous run.

  4. Run it to standard

    The facilitator runs the standard agenda; you handle logistics and keep participants in the room (or on the call). Capture attendance and photos for documentation.

  5. Report and follow through

    Submit the participant list and post-event data. For async cohorts, support the follow-up until completions land—that's what your support payment is based on.

Visibility

Recognition works both ways

Events are co-branded with ASEAN AHEAD and program partners—follow the branding guidelines and tag the official accounts in your posts. Outstanding hosts and partners are featured in program spotlights, and strong partners are the first we call for future programs and opportunities.
