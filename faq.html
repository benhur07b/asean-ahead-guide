Frequently asked questions

Quick answers, by audience.

Filter by who you are. If your question isn't here, ask through the program channels. Recurring questions get added to this page.

ParticipantsIs the training really free?
Yes. The course, certificate, and 1-year LinkedIn Premium are all free for participants. The training is organised by the ASEAN Foundation and supported by LinkedIn.
ParticipantsHow long does the course take?
Budget a minimum of 60 minutes for the core content: videos, evaluations, exercises, discussions, plus time for account setup. A guided synchronous event runs about 2.5 hours door-to-door.
ParticipantsI finished the modules — why don't I have my certificate?
Completion requires all three: 100% of the modules, at least one discussion created or joined, and the Post-Course Evaluation. The evaluation and the discussion are the two steps people miss most. Also check that your discussion post is a real contribution — see the next question.
ParticipantsWhat counts as a valid discussion post?
A meaningful, constructive contribution: a genuine insight, question, or reflection connected to any module — one or two honest sentences is enough. Joining an existing discussion counts too, and it's often the easiest way to start: read what others have posted and add your take. Random or nonsensical text (e.g. "asdf", "done", or something unrelated to the course) may invalidate your completion and the benefits tied to it. Your posts are read by other participants, so make yours worth reading — and you can keep joining or starting discussions even after you've finished the modules.
ParticipantsWhen do I get LinkedIn Premium?
Make sure you include your LinkedIn profile and an improved CV (using what you learned from the training) in the Post-Course Evaluation. After you complete the training, the ASEAN AHEAD team verifies everything internally. Verification and results take about 2–4 weeks. Keep the email on your ADA account active — that's how your completion is matched and how you'll be notified whether your LinkedIn Premium is granted or if you need to submit additional requirements.
ParticipantsCan I do the whole thing on my phone?
Yes — the course is mobile-friendly. That said, tests and discussion posts are easier on a laptop, and low-connectivity participants should consider joining a guided synchronous session.
ParticipantsI'm stuck — what do I do next?
Work through the usual suspects first:
  • A module won't mark as complete? Open its Learning Material again and go through it to the very last page — the platform only registers it once you reach the end. Do the same check for each video part.
  • Finished everything but still no certificate? Confirm all three requirements: 100% of modules, at least one discussion created or joined, and the Post-Course Evaluation.
  • Page not loading or progress not saving? Refresh, log out and back in, or switch browsers. Low connectivity? Try again on a steadier connection.
  • Still stuck? Ask your champion — they can solve most issues quickly and will escalate to the ASEAN AHEAD team if needed.
ChampionsDo I need to teach the course content?
No. You are a motivator and guide, not an instructor — the course is self-paced and teaches itself. Your job is registration, momentum, troubleshooting, and completion follow-up.
ChampionsWhat's the difference between a synchronous and asynchronous event?
Synchronous (in-person, webinar, or hybrid): the entire course is completed together during the event. Asynchronous: the event introduces the course and gets everyone registered and enrolled; you then follow up weekly until each participant completes or the cohort deadline passes.
ChampionsWhat data do I need to capture at an event?
A complete participant list: name and active email (matching the ADA account), region and institution, beneficiary type, gender and age, event details and mode, and completion status. No list, no event. Collect only these fields and follow the consent guidelines.
ChampionsWhat's the most common event-day problem?
ADA account creation and completion of at least 1 module. Budget generously for the walkthrough and get every participant registered and enrolled before the event ends, even in asynchronous mode.
PartnersCan a student organization or individual host an event?
Yes — in your school, org, barangay, or community. You follow the same standard agenda and submit the same participant list and post-event data as any other event, and qualify for host financial support when the minimum requirements are met.
PartnersWhat financial support do individual or student organization hosts get?
PhP 3,000 for every 50 participants who successfully complete the course: completion, not attendance. The event must meet all minimum requirements. You will be informed of the liquidation and disbursement requirements.
PartnersWe're a university — what do we provide vs. the program?
You bring the participants: recruitment, pre-registration, venue, and local logistics. The program brings the materials, the training, the champions, tracking and reporting, and the certificates pathway.
PartnersWhat formats can our event take?
In-person, webinar, or hybrid (synchronous — the whole course completed at the event), or an asynchronous intro session where participants finish on their own with champion follow-up.
